THE GREAT CONSTRUCTION

Chapter Eight

Part One: The Decision

February 2019. Parliament chamber. Every seat filled. Cameras broadcasting live to 45 million Ugandans and 2 billion people worldwide.

Grace Akello stood at the podium with twelve massive binders containing the Watchers' complete blueprints.

"Six people from the future died trying to save us. They failed to prevent the catastrophe. But they left us instructions for after it."

CHALLENGE TO THE READER

Your government says infrastructure takes decades. Uganda built a capital city in 24 months using 500-year-old blueprints and the audacity to try.

So what's your government's excuse? Are they incompetent? Or are they deliberately slow because someone profits from delay?

And what's YOUR excuse for accepting it?

Part Four: The Old Guard Fights Back

Not everyone celebrated. The fossil fuel companies whose business model Uganda just demolished hired lawyers, lobbyists, and mercenaries.

"They want you to believe change is impossible. Because if you believe that, you'll never try. And if you never try, they stay in power."

Part Five: Isiba Completed

January 2020. Twenty-two months after breaking ground. Two months ahead of schedule. Isiba was finished.

A city of 200,000 people. Four perfect quadrants. Clean energy, zero waste, integrated nature, transparent governance.

FINAL CHALLENGE TO THE READER

24 months. They built an entire capital city in 24 months.

What have you built in 24 months? Be specific. What tangible thing exists because you created it?

The Watchers died trying. Grace nearly died trying. Uganda transformed in 24 months by trying.

24 months. Starting now. What will you build?

Reflection

Isiba was complete. Uganda was transformed. The revolution was real.

Failure is familiar. Humans know how to handle failure. Success is terrifying. Success means you have something to lose.

The great construction was complete. Now came the great test: staying built.
